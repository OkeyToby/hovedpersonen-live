# How to Collect Story Cards

This guide helps you gather the raw material for `Tre ting, én historie` before
you enter anything in the app.

## Prerequisites

- Names for the couple.
- A list of 4-6 guests who know safe, specific stories.
- A way to contact those guests before the party.
- The app available locally so you can enter finished cards later.

## Steps

1. Pick the story owners.

   Choose guests who are comfortable speaking for 60-90 seconds. Mix family,
   friends, and generations if possible. Avoid asking only the loudest guests;
   one quiet, specific memory is often better than a broad roast.

2. Send each person a short request.

   Use this message as a starting point:

   ```text
   Hej [navn]

   Jeg laver et lille guldbryllupsindslag for [navn 1] og [navn 2].
   Vil du sende mig en kort, varm historie om dem?

   Jeg skal bruge:
   - tre ting, billeder eller ledetråde der peger mod historien
   - hvad historien handler om
   - et spørgsmål holdene kan gætte på
   - det rigtige svar

   Historien skal være familievenlig og kunne fortælles på 60-90 sekunder.
   ```

3. Turn each answer into one card.

   For every usable story, fill this worksheet:

   ```text
   Titel:
   Gæst der fortæller:
   Ting 1:
   Ting 2:
   Ting 3:
   Oplæsningsnote:
   Spørgsmål til aktivt hold:
   Korrekt svar:
   Reveal-kommentar:
   ```

4. Check the three things.

   Good things are concrete and showable:

   - an object you can hold up
   - a printed photo
   - a place name
   - a song title
   - a receipt, map, invitation, or old note

   Weak things are abstract:

   - `kærlighed`
   - `familie`
   - `en god dag`
   - `noget sjovt`

   Replace weak things with something the room can picture.

5. Make the question answerable.

   The active team should be able to guess. Prefer questions with one clear
   answer:

   - `Hvor lå cykelnøglen til sidst?`
   - `Hvilken sang fik dem på dansegulvet?`
   - `Hvor var kagen gemt?`

   Avoid questions that require private knowledge unless the clues make the
   answer guessable.

6. Add the reveal note.

   The reveal note is the warm landing after the guess. It can be:

   - a short comment from the couple
   - a kind punchline
   - a memory detail the story owner should mention
   - a cue for applause, singing, or asking the couple to confirm

7. Enter the cards in the app.

   Open [../index.html](../index.html), go to `Historiekort`, and enter one card
   at a time. Use `Tilføj kort` if you have more than the seed cases.

## Verification

Before party day, each card should pass this check:

- It has one named story owner.
- It has exactly three usable things or pictures.
- It has one question with one correct answer.
- It can be told in 60-90 seconds.
- It feels warm if read aloud in front of the whole room.

## Troubleshooting

### A guest sends a long story

Keep the best moment and turn the rest into the reveal note. The live story
should stay short enough that the room still wants the answer.

### A story is funny but risky

Rewrite it toward warmth or drop it. The couple should feel central, not exposed.

### You do not get enough stories

Use couple trivia as a fallback. Keep the same card structure: three clues, one
question, one answer, one reveal note.

### You only have photos, not objects

Print the photos or put them in a folder on the host laptop. A photo still works
as one of the three things.
