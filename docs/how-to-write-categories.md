# How to Write Quiz Categories

This guide helps you turn a chosen year into 3-7 strong categories before you
enter anything in the app.

## Prerequisites

- A target year for the quiz.
- A few minutes to recall what happened that year (music, sport, film, news,
  technology, local events).
- The app available locally so you can enter finished categories later.

## Steps

1. Pick the year.

   Choose a year the room shares a connection to — a wedding year, a birth year,
   or simply a memorable one. The finale will ask the teams to name it, so the
   categories should give enough hints to make the year guessable.

2. Choose category themes.

   Aim for a mix so different people can shine. The seed uses:

   - `Musik`
   - `Sport`
   - `Film`
   - `Nyheder`
   - `Top 3`
   - `Rundt i hjemmet`
   - `Årets nyheder`

   You can keep between 3 and 7 categories. Fewer makes a shorter show; seven is
   the maximum the board supports.

3. Write each category with this worksheet:

   ```text
   Navn:
   Spørgsmål:
   Svar:
   Forklaring:
   ```

4. Make the question answerable.

   The active team should have a fair chance. Prefer one clear answer that ties
   to the year:

   - `Hvem vandt VM i fodbold i 2002?` → `Brasilien`
   - `Hvilken ny mønt kom i omløb 1. januar 2002?` → `Euroen`

   For an open category like `Top 3`, state the judging rule in the question so
   the host knows how to score it.

5. Add an explanation.

   The explanation is the landing after the answer. Use it for a fun fact, a
   date, or a detail that helps the room feel the year. It is optional but makes
   the reveal warmer and prints on the host sheet.

6. Write the finale.

   The finale asks the room which year it is. Keep the answer the same as the
   event year. A good finale rewards teams for naming the closest year using the
   hints from the categories.

7. Enter the categories in the app.

   Open [../index.html](../index.html), stay in `Producer`, and select each
   category from the list. Use `Tilføj kategori` if you want more than the seed
   (up to 7), or `Fjern` to drop one (down to 3).

## Verification

Before party day, each category should pass this check:

- It has a short, clear `Navn`.
- It has one `Spørgsmål` with one `Svar`.
- Open questions state how they are judged.
- The readiness panel shows it counted under "kategorier med spørgsmål og svar".

The finale should have a question and the correct year as its answer.

## Troubleshooting

### The answer is too obscure

Swap it for something most of the room could place in the year. The point is a
warm, shared game, not a trivia exam.

### Two categories overlap

Merge them or pick a different theme. Seven distinct themes keep the board
interesting.

### You only have a few good questions

Run with 3-4 categories. A tight show beats a padded one.
